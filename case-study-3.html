Alchemite Analytics is a machine learning tool developed to optimise R&D in materials, chemicals, and life sciences. This case study highlights my contribution to the end-to-end process of developing the new "Projects" feature in Alchemite Analytics.

context.

Our users needed a way to manage their Models. Projects in Alchemite offer a way for users to sort their Models in a Project, very similar to a folder in a filing system.

research.

After listening to our users, we identified a recurring theme: the challenges they faced in organising their models.

collecting research.

So, how could we design a better way of managing your Models in Alchemite?

I collected research in a variety of ways from: interviewing, observing usage in person and through analytics and collecting direct requests. I also collected insights from the sales and science consultant teams to gain as much information as I could around how users Manage their Models.

customer requests.

What were our customers saying?

Not only did I interview users directly, but I also worked collaborativley with cross function teams, mainly Science, Sales and Engineering, to collate customer requests. These customer requests were entered by multiple teams through a Google form, and the results were collected from a spreadsheet. I would then collect all the insights with the product manager from the spreadsheet.

key insights.

Once collecting masses of data, both qualitative and quantitative, I needed to collate it. To do this, I collected all the insights, merging similarities and and a result defined the core problems and impact to users when managing their models.

design.

An iterative process to developing an idea for Projects which could be tested with users.

How did we develop designs?

During the design phase, it is important to me that I work closely with the engineering and science team to give them visibility on the project and the change to add a different perspective. As the only designer in the company, it is important to not dictate and allow other input.

design constraints.

ideation.

Working with the Product Trio and Engineers we were able to create user centred solutions.

problem-solving.

To kickstart the project, I hosted a product trio ideation session. The trio included the Product Manager, 2 Engineers and a Science rep. In this session, I highlighted the bounds of the project, summarising the research findings - this ensured all parties understood the problem we were trying to solve.

After the initial project summary, I asked a range of “How might we” questions, taken from Design Sprint methodologies, and asked the team to generate some ideations.

wireframes.

Once discussing the range of ideas with the trio, I was able to develop Lo-Fidelity wireframes for the most promising ideas. During this stage, I made several versions of each design, sketching and iterating to try and find the best solution. After iterating, I had come up with 2 higher-fidelity designs to start A/B testing with users.

user testing.

Understanding how our designs were experienced by our users.

usability tests.

For the first round of user testing, I set up meetings with 6 users. As a B2B company, I tried to reach out to users across different companies and roles to reduce bias.

iteration.

How did we learn from the insights gathered from user testing?

I then went through several rounds of user testing, each round testing the designs with about 5 users. In between each group I would edit the designs based of the feedback. Once at a point where users could successfully navigate the prototype, I was confident in sharing the design with the team.

feasibility meeting.

Once deciding on the final design, it was time to discuss the ideas with the engineering team and product manager. At this stage, we would discuss which mini features could and couldn’t be included in the project. This was also the time to discuss functionality in more depth and agree on high-level ways to solve these problems

customer success.

To ensure the feature was successfully adopted, I worked closely with the Product Manager to develop a customer success strategy for the adoption of “Projects”.

documentation.

Providing valuable resources for users when using the new feature.

Whilst the engineering team was developing the feature, I was able to locally host the platform and take screenshots to create thorough documentation, this would work as a guide to help users on not only how to use the new feature, but how to extract value.

video guides.

Alongside the documentation, I also created a 2 minute video tutorial highlighting from a high level how to create and use projects in Alchemite. This video was added to the docs and sent out to users in one of the marketing newsletters.

Case study showcasing the complete redesign of the Alchemite Analytics Model dashboard which gave front-end users a high-level view of their Model's performance.

Alchemite Analytics is a machine learning platoform, developed to optimise R&D in materials, chemicals and life sciences. This project showcases my contribution to the design, development and deployment of the new “Model Dashboard” feature.

context.

Extracting high level model insights from the old dashboard was challenging for our users.

purpose.

Alchemite users struggled to get a high-level overview of their model and therefore, it would take significant time and exploration to know what steps to take next. This was due to the lack of guidance in the Model’s dashboard.

user goals.

So, what did our users want to see when looking at their Model from a high-level?

After interviewing our users through semi-structured interviews and workshops, we found that when first looking at their model, they would aim to look for the following information:

  • Access total R2* quality of the Model
    •
  • View quality of target output
    •
  • Understand most important correlations of inputs to targets

our goals.

The business goal for the year was to improve customer retention to >90%. In turn, our product goal was to increase customer satisfaction and reduce the time to value for users in Alchemite Analytics.

research.

Collating our research and understanding the core needs of our users.

dashboard requirements.

What did the new dashboard need to fulfil our user’s goals?

  • Display relevant, actionable information about the user’s model
    •
  • Give users a high-level view of model performance
    •
  • Gain an understanding of how close the Model is to predicting their targets

ideation.

Developing and iterating on different designs.

lo-fi wireframes.

During the ideation stage, I worked closely with the Product Manager and Engineering team to generate and develop versions of the dashboard which would be both valuable to users and feasible to build. The initial designs were first tested internally with our scientists and then were tested with users.

information architecture.

Finding the best way to sort the IA of the Dashboard.

Once discussing the range of ideas with the trio, I was able to develop Lo-Fidelity wireframes for the most promising ideas. During this stage, I made several versions of each design, sketching and iterating to try and find the best solution. After iterating, I had come up with 2 higher-fidelity designs to start A/B testing with users.

solution.

The final result was a tile based dashboard, developed in react grid which was compatible with different screen sizes. Each tile, contains important information for the user and also acts as a navigation method around the platform.

model quality.

Valuable analytic which showcases the quality of the user’s outputs in their dataset. Green indicates qualities over 0. This tile provides a high-level view of the user’s outputs, as well as acting as a navigation mechanism, when the tile is clicked, it takes users to the full analytic.

importance heatmap.

Our most used analytic, which allows user to see the inputs with the highest correlations (most important). When a square is clicked in the plot, the user will be taken to a property vs property plot which explores that relationship further.
